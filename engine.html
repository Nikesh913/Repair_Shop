STEADY VS. FLASHING CHECK ENGINE LIGHT

If the check engine light is on but is steady rather than flashing, this means there's an issue and it's best not to ignore it. Alternatively, a flashing check engine light means the engine is misfiring and unburned fuel is going into the exhaust system. This is a serious problem that can cause damage to the catalytic converter. If the check engine light is on, an engine diagnostic is recommended to avoid costly engine repairs.

FAILING OXYGEN SENSOR

Tucked into your vehicle’s exhaust pipe there are multiple small oxygen sensors that constantly tests the exhaust system to measure the air-to-fuel ratio. The sensor transmits that data to your vehicle’s computer, which relies on this information to create an ideal air-to-fuel mixture based on the driving conditions. Symptoms of a faulty oxygen sensor aren’t obvious to a lot of motorists, so the check engine light may be the first time you become aware that a problem exists. Fixing a serious maintenance problem like a faulty oxygen sensor can improve your vehicle’s fuel efficiency by as much as 40%, according to the U.S. Department of Energy.

WHY DOES MY CHECK ENGINE LIGHT COME ON WHEN I START MY CAR?

It’s common for the check engine light to come on momentarily when you first start your vehicle. This is a normal sign that your car is testing its circuits. However, if your check engine light stays on and does not turn off, it's time to have a professional at Repairit Complete Auto Care run a diagnostic check to determine the source of the issue.
